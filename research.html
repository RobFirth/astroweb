Compact Objects

We are a leading group in the UK researching the growth and evolution of compact objects. This includes white dwarfs, neutron stars, and black holes across their full mass scale. We use multiwavelength observations to examine the relationship between the phenomena of accretion and outflows. This is bringing us closer to understanding how some of the most extreme objects in the cosmos form, grow and change with time, and how they impact their surrounding galaxies.

Time Domain Astronomy

The night sky is far from static. Accretion flows around black holes in our Galaxy vary in brightness on timespans as shorter as 1000ths of a second, much faster than the blink of an eye. Supernova explosions briefly outshine entire galaxies for a few months. And supermassive black holes generate complex variability patterns holding clues to their growth. At Southampton, we are leading large projects on a range of radio, optical, infrared and X-ray telescopes. We aim to discover new transient objects in the sky, and to use their characteristic variations to gain a deeper understanding of extreme physics, the life cycles of stars, and ultimately, measure the expansion rate of the universe.
